A common mistake is to not provide a specific value in Case 1, but only write something along the lines of mmm can be any natural number other than 000. That may be true, but it does not change the fact that the only way of proving an existential quantification is to give a concrete, well-defined witness. Any natural number other than 000 is not that.

In a more complicated proof, it can be helpful to explicitly tell your reader that you chose a witness arbitrarily within certain constraints. But for mathematical correctness, you always have to specify a specific one.
